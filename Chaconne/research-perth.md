# Research: Perth, Western Australia — Locations, Culture, Finance

## Key Details for the Novel

### The Western Suburbs — "The Golden Triangle"

Postcodes 6007–6015. Cottesloe/Peppermint Grove (postcode 6011) has the highest average taxable income in Australia: $325,343.

**Peppermint Grove**: Perth's most expensive suburb. Pop ~1,500. Median house $5M+. "Monaco of WA." Twiggy Forrest lives here.
**Dalkeith**: Three sides bounded by Swan River. Median $3.4M. Gina Rinehart.
**Cottesloe**: The beach suburb. Old money meets ocean.
**Nedlands**: Adjacent to UWA. Academic prestige + residential wealth.
**Claremont**: Commercial heart. Claremont Quarter (Zimmermann, Aje, Camilla).

### Status Markers in Perth

1. Which school (paramount)
2. Which suburb
3. Which yacht club (RFBYC = most exclusive)
4. Whether you have a boat
5. Where your holiday house is (Eagle Bay/Yallingup most prestigious)
6. Your car (European, understated. Range Rover = money. Dusty Land Cruiser = old money)
7. Wine knowledge (Margaret River: Leeuwin Estate, Vasse Felix, Cullen, Moss Wood)

### Schools

**Scotch College**: Independent Uniting Church boys' school, Swanbourne. Founded 1897. ~1,900 boys. Fees ~$28,180/yr. Old Scotch Collegians: 16,500+ members with formal Business Directory. Scottish heritage (tartan ties, pipe band). Sister school: PLC. Shuttle buses run between campuses in Years 11–12 — this is how Scotch boys and PLC girls meet and eventually marry.

**PLC (Presbyterian Ladies' College)**: Independent Uniting Church girls' school, **Peppermint Grove**. Founded 1915. ~1,200 students. Fees ~$35,000/yr — **most expensive school in Perth**. Blackwatch tartan, pipe band. The PLC girl stereotype: confident, well-spoken, expected to attend UWA.

**The classic pairing**: Scotch boy + PLC girl = the western suburbs match.

### Napoleon Street, Cottesloe

~250 metres. Village heart of Cottesloe. Beachside village feel. Van's Cafe, Cimbalino, Sal's Pasta Deli. Boutiques: FLANNEL (bias-cut slip dresses), Picnic (30+ year local label), Boheme Luxe, Shiraz. **A fashion boutique here makes perfect sense for the character.**

### St George's Terrace — "The Terrace"

Main CBD street. Glass towers: BHP, Rio Tinto, Fortescue offices. Law firms, accounting firms, stockbrokers. QV1 tower at 250 (Euroz Hartleys on Level 37). Palace Hotel dates from 1897 gold rush.

### Cottesloe Beach

1.5km white sand. **Terraced grass** stepping down from Marine Parade to sand, shaded by Norfolk Island pines. The Indiana (landmark restaurant, 1996 build). Cottesloe Beach Hotel ("The Cott") for Sunday sessions. Faces west — legendary sunsets, sky turns copper over the Indian Ocean. The Fremantle Doctor (afternoon sea breeze) saves it from 40°C+ heat.

**For the novel's ending**: Bob walks across the grass (the terraced lawns between the houses on Marine Parade and the beach), out to the sand, and into the ocean. The proximity of comfortable domestic life to the sea is Cottesloe's defining feature.

### Rottnest Island ("Rotto")

18km offshore. No cars. Ferries from Fremantle (30 min) or by private boat. Famous for quokkas. Thomson Bay (main settlement, 33 moorings for boats 8–40m), Longreach Bay, Geordie Bay. Moorings pre-booked up to 100 days in advance — scarcity is a status marker.

**The Rotto weekend**: Leave Friday afternoon, motor across (1–2 hrs in a Riviera 48), pick up mooring, chardonnay on the flybridge, raft-up with friends' boats Saturday, motor home Sunday.

### Boating — The Riviera 48

Australian-made motor yacht (not sailing). 48 feet, twin Cat diesels, three cabins, flybridge. 169 units made 1993–2004. Second-hand $200K–$500K+. Annual running cost $30K–$50K.

**What it signals**: Established, comfortable wealth. Not obscene (super-rich own 60ft+ Sunseeker/Princess). Perfect for a stockbroker partner. Riviera owners form a community — 45+ motor yachts have rafted up together on the Swan River.

**Yacht clubs**: Royal Freshwater Bay YC (Peppermint Grove, most exclusive, membership requires proposer/seconder of 5+ years), Royal Perth YC (Crawley), Fremantle Sailing Club.

### Stockbroking in Perth

Always inseparable from mining. Key firms: Euroz Hartleys (WA's largest, Level 37 QV1), Patersons Securities (est. 1903, sold to Canaccord 2019), Argonaut, Petra Capital.

**The shift**: Commission-based broking (2% per trade in early '90s) collapsed with online trading. Survivors pivoted to wealth/funds management. Boutique one-man funds are common in Perth — family offices tripled 2019–2023.

**Perth financial scandals**:
- **WA Inc** (1980s): Corrupt government–business partnerships. $600M+ lost. Laurie Connell jailed.
- **Alan Bond**: America's Cup hero → Australia's biggest corporate fraud ($1.2B). Jailed 1997.
- **Chris Marco** (2013–2018): Perth Ponzi scheme. $250M from 151 investors. Sentenced to **14 years** in September 2025. 43 fraud counts.
- **Westpoint/Norm Carey**: $304M from 3,524 investors. Ponzi structure. Financial planners got 10% upfront commissions.

**ASIC investigation process**: Tip-off → preliminary inquiry → formal investigation (compulsory notices, examinations under oath) → asset freezes → search warrants with AFP → licence suspension → criminal referral to CDPP → trial. Can take years.

### Perth Culture

- **Isolation**: Most isolated city of 1M+ people in the world. Nearest comparable city (Adelaide) is 2,000+ km. This makes social networks tight and inescapable. "There's no escape from your social circle."
- **Lifestyle**: Beach, boats, wine, outdoors. 8+ hrs sunshine/day average. Alfresco dining is default.
- **Mining cycle**: Boom 2003–2011 (iron ore $32→$180/ton), bust 2012–2016 (64,000 jobs lost), recovery 2017+.
- **"Larrikin"**: Mischievous, cheeky, anti-authoritarian but lovable. Originally "lout" — evolved to admiration.
- **Social conservatism**: Conformity to lifestyle (outdoors, sports, drinking). Enforced through inclusion/exclusion, not confrontation.
- **Drinking**: Central to social life. Sunday sessions. Margaret River wines. A bottle with Tuesday lunch raises no eyebrows.
