# CHACONNE — Writing Plan

## Overview
Literary novel. ~87,000 words. 31 chapters (30 + coda) in 4 acts with 3 historical interludes. Set in Perth, Western Australia and Austria. Central subject: Bach's Chaconne from Partita No. 2 in D minor, BWV 1004.

## Completed Work
- [x] Raw dictation transcribed and understood
- [x] Deep research: Bach/Chaconne, Stradivarius, Perth, violin pedagogy
- [x] Character biographies: Bob, Jeannie, Chase, Shanel, Franz (luthier), Katarina (teacher)
- [x] Historical biographies: Bach, Maria Barbara, Stradivari
- [x] Chapter structure (31 chapters with summaries and word counts)

## Writing Process — 4 Waves

### WAVE 1: Scene-Level Outline
**Goal**: Expand every chapter from 2-3 sentence summary to a full page of scene beats.
**What each chapter expansion includes**:
- Every scene in the chapter (location, characters present, time of day/year)
- What happens beat by beat
- Key dialogue moments (not full dialogue — just what needs to be said)
- What the reader learns or feels
- What is being set up for later chapters (plants and payoffs)
- POV and any structural notes (flashbacks, time jumps)
- Threads active in this chapter (fund status, violin progress, Jeannie's state, kids' arcs, authentication)

**Output**: `Chaconne/wave1-scene-outline.md` (~15,000 words)

**Structural checks during Wave 1**:
- Does the fund's collapse accelerate at the right pace? (Should feel like slow creep in Act I, background tension in Act II, approaching disaster in Act III, detonation in Act IV)
- Does Jeannie's drinking/deterioration escalate believably?
- Does Bob's violin progress match the pedagogy research? (Months 1-3 despair, 6-12 plateau, 12-18 breakthrough, 18-24 mastery-of-sorts)
- Does the Strad authentication subplot have enough tension and plausibility?
- Are the historical interludes placed at structurally resonant moments?
- Does every chapter earn its place? Cut any that don't.

### WAVE 2: Voice and Texture
**Goal**: Write the opening 500-800 words of every chapter to lock in prose style, POV feel, and rhythm before committing to full drafts.
**What this establishes**:
- Narrative voice (third person limited? Whose head are we in?)
- Prose register (literary but accessible — Tolstoy-inspired psychological depth, not experimental)
- Tonal variation between chapters (Perth social comedy vs. musical transcendence vs. historical gravity)
- How the historical interludes differ in voice from the contemporary narrative
- How internal monologue works (free indirect discourse? Direct thought?)

**Output**: `Chaconne/wave2-openings.md` (~20,000 words, ~650 words × 31 chapters)

**Voice checks during Wave 2**:
- Is the voice consistent across contemporary chapters?
- Do the historical interludes feel distinct but not jarring?
- Is the Perth voice specific enough? (Should feel like it could only be this city)
- Is the music writing good? (The passages about playing violin must feel physically real)
- Is Jeannie's cruelty shown through action/dialogue, not just narrated?

### WAVE 3: Pivotal Scenes (Full Draft)
**Goal**: Write the 8-10 scenes that carry the most narrative and emotional weight, in full, before filling in around them. These are the load-bearing walls.

**The pivotal scenes** (in approximate narrative order):
1. **Bob in the violin shop with Franz** (Ch 11) — the encounter, the instrument, the purchase, the grace
2. **Bob hears the Chaconne for the first time** (Ch 17) — the cliff face moment, pulling over in the car or finding it on YouTube
3. **The first lesson with Katarina** (Ch 15) — she hears what he is, the Chaconne request, her laugh then her agreement
4. **Bob plays the opening chord for the first time** (Ch 17 or 19) — the physical reality of trying to play this piece
5. **The breakthrough** (Ch 22) — the day it starts to speak, polyphony reveals itself
6. **The Strad confirmation** (Ch 23) — the email/call on his birthday morning
7. **The birthday performance** (Ch 27) — the full Chaconne, described in real time, the crowd's reaction, each family member's response
8. **Jeannie smashes the violin** (Ch 29) — the shriek, the act, the aftermath
9. **The beach** (Ch 30) — Bob walks out, swims, the ambiguity
10. **The recording** (Coda) — Katarina alone, the phone, the silence after

**Output**: `Chaconne/wave3-pivotal-scenes/` (directory with one file per scene, ~25,000-30,000 words total)

**Scene checks during Wave 3**:
- Does the violin shop scene earn its spiritual weight without being sentimental?
- Does the birthday performance work as the novel's climax? (Must sustain 3,000+ words of a man playing violin — the hardest scene to write)
- Does the smashing feel inevitable, not melodramatic?
- Is the ending ambiguous enough? (Not clearly suicide, not clearly accident)
- Does the coda land? (The recording as the only surviving trace)

### WAVE 4: Full Draft (Linear)
**Goal**: Write every chapter in order, incorporating the Wave 2 openings and Wave 3 pivotal scenes, filling in everything between them.

**Process**:
- Write one chapter at a time, in order
- Each chapter drafted in full, aiming for the word count in the structure document
- After every ~5 chapters, pause and re-read for consistency, pacing, voice
- Track thread continuity (a running note of: fund status, violin skill level, Jeannie's state, calendar/timeline, what each character knows)

**Output**: `Chaconne/draft/` (directory with one file per chapter, building toward the full ~87,000 word manuscript)

**Continuity tracking during Wave 4**:
- Timeline: What month/year is each chapter? Does the 2-year practice timeline work?
- Fund: What's the fund's actual financial position at each point?
- Violin: What can Bob actually play at this point? (Must match pedagogy research)
- Jeannie: How much is she drinking? What does she know?
- Kids: How old are they? What's happening in their lives?
- Authentication: Where is the provenance research at each point?

## Execution Order

1. **Wave 1**: Scene-level outline for all 31 chapters
2. **Wave 2**: Opening 500-800 words of each chapter
3. **Wave 3**: 10 pivotal scenes in full
4. Review Waves 1-3 with Jon — adjust structure, voice, scenes before committing to full draft
5. **Wave 4**: Full linear draft, chapter by chapter
6. Revision pass: continuity, voice consistency, pacing
7. Final polish

## Notes
- The novel's working title is **Chaconne**
- Voice: third person limited, primarily Bob's POV, with selected chapters from Jeannie's, Chase's, and Shanel's perspectives
- Historical interludes in a slightly more elevated, formal register
- Perth must feel specific and real — use actual street names, actual schools (or lightly fictionalized), actual culture
- The music writing must feel physically authentic — use the pedagogy research
- The fund subplot must feel legally plausible — use the Chris Marco / Westpoint research
- The Strad/authentication subplot must feel expert — use the Stradivarius research
- Tone: literary realism with psychological depth. Tolstoy is the aspiration. Dark comedy in the social scenes. Transcendence in the music scenes. Ambiguity at the end.
